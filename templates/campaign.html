Who we are

New Zealand has a great record in Underwater Hockey on the international stage. Currently, we are the number one country in the world with five of the eight world championship titles. The Age Group teams (U24 & U18) are currently working on their campaign for the 6th CMAS Underwater Hockey Age Group Championships to be held in July / August 2022 (dates to be confirmed). This will also include the Asia Pacific Cup which is scheduled to be held in the Gold Coast in September 2023.

Cam's Top Camp and Game Tips :)

As a player you are expected to bring an open mind, a willingness to learn, a kind and friendly manner, and the determination to push yourself through some difficult times. Training for an international competition is not always easy, and you will rely on each other to help push through the tough times. Knowing someone else is getting out of bed early to do a swim session makes it that little bit easier to do yourself ;-).

As competitive athletes please remember that we need to strive to be the best whilst being humble, kind and respectful to our squad mates. Underwater Hockey is a team sport. Without a supportive, cohesive team around us, we can't be amazing UWH players! Sometimes our competitiveness can see us being critical and making comments that may hurt others and within this squad we want everyone to enjoy the campaign experience so remember to ensure you bring a positive mindset to the camps.

For those players born in 2006 you may begin training with the U24 squad however, you will still be considered for the U19 squad as well with Ben Forman the U24 coach and myself working together to ensure we are working towards the best outcome for all players.
